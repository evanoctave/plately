# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Health-conscious adults (20s–30s) logging nutrition daily, often right after eating, on the go. Primary job: quickly log a meal, immediately understand how the day is going. Using it in good lighting, often standing in a kitchen or sitting at a desk. Not anxious — just checking in.

## Product Purpose

Photo-first nutrition tracker that makes daily logging feel fast and satisfying. Success is habit formation: the app should be something people open without obligation, because it feels good to use.

## Brand Personality

Sharp, clean, satisfying. Precise without being clinical. Data that looks good. Every interaction confirms it worked.

## Anti-references

- Soft pastel wellness (beige, sage, spa aesthetic — too passive, too low-stakes)
- Overly gamified (excessive badges, streaks, confetti — feels manipulative)
- Generic dark fitness app (dark navy + neon green — every macro tracker looks this way)
- Clinical database apps (MyFitnessPal, Lose It — functional but joyless, data-dumping)

## Design Principles

1. Precision feels good: data should be crisp, legible, and satisfying to read. Never approximate or soften what should be exact.
2. Color earns its place: use color to signal meaning (progress, state, macro type). Not decoration.
3. Motion confirms action: animations confirm something happened or is happening. Not choreography, not filler.
4. Remove the scaffolding: cards and containers only where they provide structural meaning. Naked content on the background is often better.
5. Daily return matters: the home screen must feel rewarding to open. Not dopamine-hacking, just genuinely well-made.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA contrast minimum. Reduced motion: respect system preference where feasible.
